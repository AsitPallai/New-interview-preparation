Modern Air Brake System — Step wise Air Flow

  1. Air Generation & Cleaning
    • Engine drives Air Compressor → compresses atmospheric air.
    • Compressed air goes to the Air Dryer (removes moisture, oil, dust).
    • Then air is stored in the Purge Tank for regeneration of dryer.

  2. Distribution of Air
    • From the dryer, air goes to the Quick Service Protection Valve (QSPV).
    • QSPV distributes air to:
      • Front Air Tank
      • Rear Air Tank
      • Parking/Accessory Air Tank
    • This ensures that if one circuit fails, the others remain functional (safety feature).

  3. Brake Application System
    • From front & rear air tanks, air goes to the Dual Brake Valve (Foot Brake Valve).
    • When the driver presses the brake pedal:
      • The Dual Brake Valve sends controlled air pressure to Brake Chambers via sensing and delivery lines.
      • Quick Release Valve (QRV)is used for fast release of air from chambers, so brakes release quickly.

  4. Emergency & Parking Brake System
    • Air from Parking Brake Tank goes through the Parking Brake Valve.
    • It controls the Spring Brake Actuators:
      • When air is supplied → spring is compressed → brake released.
      • When air is cut off (parking position or failure) → spring pushes → brake applied automatically (fail-safe).

  5. Safety Valves & Regulators
    • RG2 Valve:Balances rear axle brake force (prevents skidding).
    • Air Pressure Gauge: Shows pressure in tanks for driver monitoring.
    • QSPV (Quick Supply Protection Valve): Prevents loss of air in one circuit from draining other circuits.

  6. Accessories
    • Extra Air Tank also supplies air for horns, doors, suspension, or auxiliary systemsin vehicle.

Flow Summary

  1. Compressor → Air Dryer → Purge Tank → QSPV → Storage Tanks.
  2. From tanks → Dual Brake Valve → Brake Chambers (service braking).
  3. Parking Valve → Spring Brake Actuators (parking/emergency braking).
  4. Safety ensured by QRV, RG2, and gauges.

In short method to remember

In Tata BS6 tippers, the braking works with a pneumatic air system. First, the engine-driven compressor builds air and stores it in the reservoirs through the dryer. When I press the brake pedal, the foot valve sends controlled air pressure to the relay valves, which quickly deliver air to the brake chambers near the wheels. In the chambers, the air pressure pushes the diaphragm and moves the pushrod. This pushrod rotates the slack adjuster and S-cam, which applies the brake shoes or pads on the drum or disc. The friction slows the wheel and the vehicle stops. When I release the pedal, the valve exhausts the air, the springs pull back the shoes, and the brakes are released. For safety, ABS, low-pressure warning, and spring brakes are also provided.”
